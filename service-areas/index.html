Why Central Valley Roofs Fail Faster

The San Joaquin Valley climate is uniquely hard on asphalt shingles. Here's what's working against your roof right now.

Extreme Summer Heat

Fresno regularly hits 105–110°F in July and August. Roof surfaces can reach 150°F+. That daily thermal expansion and contraction cracks sealant, loosens flashing, and bakes the oils out of asphalt shingles faster than coastal California climates — accelerating aging by 30–50%.

Intense UV Exposure

The Central Valley averages over 300 sunny days per year with very few coastal clouds to filter UV radiation. UV is the single biggest driver of shingle granule loss — once granules go, the asphalt beneath degrades rapidly. Roofs here lose 5–10 years of lifespan compared to cloudier climates.

Dry Season / Wet Season Cycle

Six months of no rain followed by concentrated winter storms is a tough cycle for any roof system. Shingles that dried and cracked over summer are suddenly asked to shed heavy rain. Compromised flashing and brittle shingles let water in fast — often before the homeowner knows there's a problem.
